Geo Owl · Information Environment Analyst
Russell Dudek
Map the delta before it becomes the decision.

Entry Principle

Build mission fluency through controlled repetitions, then expand speed and ownership.

The first 90 days reduce uncertainty about mission standards, source quality, local context, reporting expectations, and the boundaries of LLM assistance. The sequence favors rapid learning, visible calibration, and measurable reliability.

Days 1-15 · Orient to Mission and Standards

Learn

  • JCO tasking rhythm, shift expectations, acknowledgment rules, and Site Lead escalation preferences.
  • Required SITREP, Rapid Recap, AAR, database, and machine-to-machine submission formats.
  • Source restrictions, approved tools, handling expectations, and collaboration boundaries.
  • How operators distinguish routine variation, uncertainty, risk, and action-worthy change.

Produce

  • Personal mission glossary and product checklist.
  • Draft decision-cue template with confidence, provenance, relevance, and next collection.
  • Observed improvement register for sources, language, automation, and handoffs.
  • Shadow products reviewed against experienced analyst output.

Days 16-30 · Build the Localized Baseline

Map the terrain

  • Regional sources, hard-to-access channels, keywords, spelling variants, language variants, hashtags, recurring events, and known actors.
  • Normal narrative families, sentiment ranges, discourse patterns, content velocity, and audience-response behavior.
  • Source reliability, independence, local legitimacy, latency, and blind spots.

Validate the baseline

  • Compare public signals with operator and partner feedback where permitted.
  • Document ambiguity and alternative explanations.
  • Define provisional change indicators and confidence thresholds.
  • Review baseline assumptions with the Site Lead and experienced analysts.

Days 31-60 · Operate Dual-Track

Manual analytical track

  • Collect, validate, interpret, and write products using the approved analyst process.
  • Preserve source traceability and state uncertainty directly.
  • Record why a cue was issued, held, expanded, or escalated.

LLM-assisted track

  • Use approved LLM tools for extraction, structuring, comparison, and draft acceleration.
  • Compare manual and assisted products for latency, omissions, unsupported claims, context loss, and correction burden.
  • Document every material manual override and the rule it suggests.
Gate: automation earns broader use where it improves speed without degrading provenance, localization, confidence discipline, or analyst accountability.

Days 61-90 · Own the Shift and Learning Loop

  • Manage assigned taskers with continuous operational presence and visible timing commitments.
  • Deliver concise, decision-ready SITREPs, Rapid Recaps, and AARs within defined windows.
  • Use recurring false positives, missed signals, source gaps, and override reasons to update the baseline and workflow.
  • Propose bounded improvements tied to measured analyst friction or operator value.
  • Demonstrate when to operate manually because the scenario exceeds automated capability.
