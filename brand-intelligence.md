# Geo Owl Brand Intelligence

## Official company sources

- Official site: https://www.geoowl.com/
- Careers and open positions: https://www.geoowl.com/careers/
- Official site visual reference: dark navy operational surfaces, bright cyan recognition accents, white space, topographic and intelligence-system imagery.

## Visible company identity

The campaign uses the company name `GEO OWL` as nominative identification and pairs it with `Candidate Vision` or `Independent candidate work` so the site cannot be mistaken for an official Geo Owl publication.

## Color token provenance

Source-sampled from Geo Owl's public web identity:

- Cyan recognition accent: `#00ABEB`
- Graphite neutral: `#59595B`
- White: `#FFFFFF`
- Deep operational navy: `#07131D` and `#0C2534`

Candidate-original operational accents:

- Signal amber: `#F5C84C`
- Alert coral: `#F05D5E`
- Readiness green: `#55C58F`

## Typography decision

Geo Owl's public site uses a bold, modern, high-impact sans-serif character. The campaign uses the licensed local/system stack `"Avenir Next", "Segoe UI", Tahoma, sans-serif` to reproduce the hierarchy, weight, spacing, and operational density without copying proprietary font files.

## Geometry and interface cues

- Topographic contours
- Source nodes and directional vectors
- Dark intelligence-workspace surfaces
- Cyan signal paths
- Compact decision readouts
- Clear readiness and action states

## Motion character

The Narrative Delta Map uses slow contour drift and signal pulses to show change propagation. Scenario controls update the recommended operator posture. Reduced-motion settings remove continuous animation while preserving every state and control.

## Artifact application

- Candidate vision: fullest operational visual system and interactive Narrative Delta Map
- Resume and cover letter: restrained cyan rules, navy typography, mission-specific identity line
- Interview brief, entry plan, and mission readiness brief: consulting-style document grid with the same decision and readiness vocabulary

## Independent-candidate distinction

Every public surface identifies the work as Russell Dudek's independent candidate material and includes a non-endorsement statement.
