Geo Owl Hiring Team Re: Information Environment Analyst - U.S. Space Force Joint Commercial Operations

The hard part of this role is not collecting more public information. It is determining what changed, whether the change is credible and operationally relevant, and what an operator should do before the picture shifts again. That is the problem I would be joining to solve.

My background is adjacent rather than conventional. I have not held an Information Environment Analyst billet, and I do not claim professional tenure in regional sentiment analysis, foreign-language discourse monitoring, or operational SITREP production. If Geo Owl requires fully independent OSINT/PAI tradecraft on day one, that gap is material. I would rather name it clearly than disguise it with inflated terminology.

The transferability lies in how I have operated. At Amazon, I led execution in a 24/7 environment supporting approximately five million second-day orders annually, using visible cadence, standard work, and escalation paths. At ZeusVu, I led regulated geospatial and aerial-data operations across restricted and high-consequence environments with a 100% safety record and no FAA incidents. At Compunetics, I led high-reliability technical programs serving customers including the Department of Defense. Today at Vape-Jet, I connect factory, field, customer, engineering, and machine-fleet signals into earlier risk detection and prioritized action.

I also design AI-assisted operating workflows through DudeWorth. The model accelerates extraction, organization, comparison, and drafting; the analyst remains accountable for provenance, cultural nuance, confidence, evaluation, and manual override. That is the posture I would bring to LLM-assisted SITREP production.

The accompanying candidate vision makes this reasoning inspectable through a Narrative Delta Protocol, an interactive change-detection model, a hard-objection analysis, and a 90-day entry plan. It is not a claim to know Geo Owl's undisclosed process. It is a concrete hypothesis for how I would learn the mission, prove judgment through controlled repetitions, and earn trust on shift.

I would welcome a candid conversation about the training runway, source environment, tradecraft expectations, and what the JCO Site Lead considers a decision-useful product.
