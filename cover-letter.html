Geo Owl Hiring Team Re: Information Environment Analyst - U.S. Space Force Joint Commercial Operations

The hard part of this role is not collecting more public information. It is determining what changed, whether the change is credible and operationally relevant, and what an operator should do before the picture shifts again. That is the problem I am prepared to help solve.

I bring a combination that maps strongly to that mandate: high-tempo operating discipline, regulated geospatial field experience, defense-linked technical rigor, multi-source signal integration, and hands-on design of human-governed AI workflows.

At Amazon, I led execution in a 24/7 environment supporting approximately five million second-day orders annually, using visible cadence, standard work, and escalation paths. At ZeusVu, I led regulated geospatial and aerial-data operations across restricted and high-consequence environments with a 100% safety record and no FAA incidents. At Compunetics, I led high-reliability technical programs serving customers including the Department of Defense. Today at Vape-Jet, I connect factory, field, customer, engineering, and machine-fleet signals into earlier risk detection and prioritized action.

Through DudeWorth, I also design LLM and agentic workflows that preserve structured context, source grounding, human review, evaluation, escalation, and override authority. That is the posture I would bring to LLM-assisted SITREP production: use the model to accelerate extraction, comparison, organization, and drafting while keeping the analyst accountable for provenance, cultural nuance, confidence, and operational relevance.

My career path is not a conventional IE analyst path; it is a highly relevant operating path into the mission. The focused ramp is formal PAI/OSINT product conventions, mission-specific source architecture, and regional language context. The accompanying candidate vision and 90-day plan make that ramp concrete through a Narrative Delta Protocol, a dual-track manual/LLM workflow, and measurable proof standards.

I would welcome the opportunity to demonstrate the transfer through a realistic baseline and change-detection exercise, and to discuss what the JCO Site Lead considers a decision-useful product.
