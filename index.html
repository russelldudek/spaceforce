Independent candidate vision by Russell Dudek

Map the delta before it becomes the decision.

The mission is not merely to collect more public information. It is to establish what “normal” looks like in a localized information environment, detect meaningful deviation early, validate it without stripping away cultural context, and give operators a concise cue they can act on.

The real mandate

Turn change into operator advantage.

Geo Owl’s analyst must connect planning, execution, and assessment—not produce disconnected research products. The role begins with a defensible baseline and ends with an operational learning loop.

Baseline the information terrain. Detect the change. Preserve provenance and context. Cue the operator. Learn from what happened next.

SpeedTaskers acknowledged within 30 minutes and live changes surfaced early.
AccuracyFast reporting without converting weak signals into false certainty.
AutomationLLMs accelerate structure and drafting; analysts retain judgment and override authority.
LocalizationRegional language, actors, norms, and hard-to-access sources matter more than generic volume.
ContinuityPlanning baselines, live SITREPs, Rapid Recaps, and AARs must share one evidence chain.
Narrative Delta Protocol

Five controls between signal and decision.

01

Baseline

Map normal narratives, sentiment ranges, discourse, key actors, recurring events, source access, and known blind spots.

02

Detect

Track velocity, repetition, source diversity, network coordination, anomaly patterns, and audience-behavior change.

03

Corroborate

Test provenance, language nuance, cultural context, source independence, timing, and alternative explanations.

04

Cue

State what changed, confidence, operational relevance, immediate collection need, and recommended operator posture.

05

Recalibrate

Use Rapid Recaps and AARs to update baselines, thresholds, source maps, prompts, and manual-override rules.

“Where is the direct OSINT / PAI tradecraft?”

The objection is valid.

Russell’s verified evidence does not establish professional sentiment analysis, regional narrative monitoring, SOUTHCOM language proficiency, or prior SITREP/AAR production in an information-environment billet. This is a deliberate adjacent-fit campaign, not a disguised direct fit.

The reason to continue the conversation would be the combination of high-tempo operational discipline, geospatial field experience, defense-linked technical work, and hands-on LLM workflow governance. The reason to stop would be if the contract requires immediate independent tradecraft with no training runway. Both conclusions are legitimate.

First 90 days

Earn trust through controlled repetitions.

Days 1–15

Orient to mission and standards

Learn tasking rhythms, source restrictions, reporting formats, escalation thresholds, database schemas, and human-authority expectations.

Days 16–30

Build the localized baseline

Curate sources, keywords, language variants, key actors, recurring narratives, normal sentiment ranges, and blind spots.

Days 31–60

Operate dual-track

Produce manual and LLM-assisted drafts in parallel; measure latency, correction rate, provenance quality, and override reasons.

Days 61–90

Own the shift and learning loop

Deliver decision-ready products, close recaps and AARs on time, and turn recurring failures into improved baselines and controls.

Questions worth asking

Discover the real operating contract.

  1. What operator decision should a live IE product change—and what does a useless update look like?
  2. Which failure is more damaging here: an early false cue or a missed narrative shift?
  3. How is the baseline established when public signals conflict with partner-nation feedback?
  4. Where are language and cultural-context gaps currently covered, and where are they not?
  5. How are LLM outputs logged, validated, corrected, and overridden?
  6. What distinguishes an excellent Rapid Recap from a merely complete one?
  7. Which machine-to-machine submission standards create the most analyst friction?
  8. What would make the Site Lead trust an analyst to own a shift by day 60?
